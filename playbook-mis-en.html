Module 01

Access, internationalization & your logistics hub

The system is your virtual office. It connects real needs in the field with donors around the world.

1.1 — Authentication screen (login)

Action

Enter your registered primary email and password on the login screen.

Context

Your email is your security key and audit trail. All operations performed under this login are your sole responsibility to the organization. Always use your primary registered email.

1.2 — Internationalization (languages)

Action

Use the language selector in the menu to change the panel language.

Context

Since the platform operates across multiple countries, manage your panel in the language you are most fluent in — this prevents misinterpretation errors, especially when filling out beneficiary registrations.

1.3 — Your profile (the foundation of operations)

Action

Access "My Profile" in the top menu. This is where your missionary data, background, and credibility documents are stored.

Context

Attached documents verify your integrity to the organization and institutional donors. A complete, documented profile builds trust and can speed up the approval of your operations. Keep it always up to date.

Missionary Profile

This section displays your missionary identification data — your name, active region, registration status, and the documents that prove your credentials to the organization and to institutional donors. A complete profile increases the speed at which your operations are approved and demonstrates accountability to funders reviewing your track record.

!

Golden logistics rule #01 — The delivery address

The address registered in your profile is the only address recognized by the system for physical shipments.

How it works: We do NOT ship Bibles to the beneficiary's home. Regardless of where the beneficiary lives, all funded Bibles and Audio Bibles will be dispatched to your address — no exceptions.

Your role: You act as a local logistics hub. You receive the package, store it temporarily, and make the final delivery (last mile) directly into each beneficiary's hands.

ZIP / Postal code is correct?
Street name and number are correct?
Complement is filled in (apartment, block, landmark)?

An error here means lost Bibles — with no possibility of recovery.

Module 03

In the top menu, there are three fundamental tabs. It is vital not to confuse them — each has a completely different function and a different moment of use in daily operations.

  • Field to upload an extra testimonial video (speeds up funding)
Tab 02
Requests
Waiting room / Room of miracles

The most important tab in the panel. Only funded requests appear here. An operation exists in this tab only when a request has been paid for.

Operation Profile

If the Bible has arrived and you need to record the video, you will always be in this tab to upload it. Never in the others.

Module 04

Beneficiary registration — the intake funnel

This is the moment you enter a new life into the system. Every piece of data collected here feeds the organization's database and directly affects the logistics of production and delivery.

Step 4.1 — Basic personal data
First & last name
Shorten if very long, but ensure the person is clearly and uniquely identified in the system.
Date of birth
Fill in day, month, and year. Used to calculate age and for future birthday gift campaigns.
Tip: If the person doesn't know their exact birth year — common in remote areas — estimate it from their appearance and use a default day and month. What matters is having an approximate age reference.
Gender
Select Male or Female. Required field for statistical reporting.
National ID / CPF
In other countries, use the equivalent identity document — passport, ID card, etc. Not a blocker — registration can be completed without it.
Step 4.1b — Age Group & Language
Is the beneficiary a child or an adult?
Select Child or Adult before proceeding.
Context: This selection directly determines which Bible format will be produced. If the beneficiary is a child, the Children's Audio Bible will be applied automatically — no further selection is needed.

Rule: Child selected → Children's Audio Bible is automatically assigned. Adult selected → product is determined by reading ability (see Step 4.4).

Beneficiary's Language
Search and select the language spoken by the beneficiary so the Bible can be made available in the correct language.
Context: The platform supports Bibles in multiple languages. Selecting the correct language ensures the beneficiary receives a version they can actually understand and use. Use the search field to find the language — do not guess or leave it blank.

Tip: If the person speaks a regional dialect or a less common language, select the closest official language and add a note in the Additional Information field.

Beneficiary's Contact Phone
If the beneficiary has their own phone number, enter it here to enable direct contact.

Note: This is separate from the reference contact. If the beneficiary has a phone, registering it here allows the organization and you to reach them directly when needed. If they do not have one, leave this field blank and rely on the reference contact registered in Step 4.2.

Step 4.2 — Contact & location engineering
Has contact? (Yes/No)
Note: Weeks may pass between registration and the Bible arriving at your address. If the beneficiary has no phone, register a neighbor's, close relative's, or local church leader's number. You need a reliable way to reach them when the material arrives. This contact does not need to be the beneficiary themselves.
Beneficiary's address
Where they sleep. This is strictly for you — the missionary — to plan your local delivery route. The product will always be shipped to your profile address, never to the beneficiary's. If the beneficiary has no fixed address, select No and use the reference contact to locate them.
WhatsApp Contact
If the beneficiary or their reference contact uses WhatsApp, register the number here.
Tip: WhatsApp is often the most reliable way to reach people in remote areas. A quick message with a location pin or a voice note can save hours of searching when it is time to deliver.

Step 4.4 — Socioeconomics & the "product rule"
Household members
How many people live under the same roof? Measures indirect impact — a single Bible delivered to a household head can transform the lives of 5, 8, or 10 people at once.
Annual income range

Reading ability

Pay close attention when filling out this field — it determines what will be manufactured and shipped.

Module 05

The accountability video — the heart of the operation

The donation does not end when the beneficiary receives the Bible; it ends when the funder watches the video. Without this cycle closure, the trust system breaks and your profile may be blocked from generating new operations.

5.1 — The purpose and core objective of the video
🛡

Proof of life (absolute compliance)

This is irrefutable proof that the donor's money was not misused, that the Bible reached the correct destination, and that the registered beneficiary is a real person. Without this video, the donation has no legal or moral verification.

♡

The emotional receipt

For someone donating from the other side of the world, seeing the smile, hearing the voice, and witnessing the beneficiary's environment is the greatest possible reward. This is exactly what motivates donors to keep funding your project.

★

Your credibility & hidden score

Missionaries who send well-directed, clear, and authentic videos earn a hidden trust score with donors. Your next requests will be funded much faster than those from missionaries who send low-quality or no videos.

5.2 — Preparation (before pressing REC)

"Don't show up recording by surprise. The video must convey dignity, care, and authenticity."

1

In the system — before leaving for the field

Open the Operations tab, click on the delivery you will make, and write down the exact names of the funders.

There may be 1, 2, or up to 3 names — this is called Split Funding, when several people share the cost of a Bible. You will need to say all names in the video. Never omit any of them.

2

With the beneficiary — before recording

Explain what is about to happen before turning on the camera. Use this approach:

"This Bible was paid for by people who love God and want to bless you. I need to record a quick thank-you video to send to them. Is that okay?"

This puts the person at ease, creates positive expectation, and prepares them emotionally for the recording moment. Never surprise the beneficiary with the camera already rolling.

5.2b — The Video Session and Upload Flow

Within the operation profile, there is a dedicated section called Video Session. This is where the missionary manages everything related to the delivery proof video for that specific operation.

How the Video Session Works

The Video Session is the final step inside each operation. It is where you submit visual proof that the delivery took place. You will find a clearly labeled button to add or upload a video directly from this screen.

When you click the upload button, a pop-up window will appear. From there, you can record directly via your device's camera or select an existing video file from your gallery. After selecting the file, confirm the upload and wait for the system to process it.

Upon successful video upload, the operation status updates automatically — the system closes the delivery loop and sends a notification to the funder with the link to watch the video.

The Missionary's Role

As a missionary, you are both the Director and Presenter of each delivery video. You are responsible for planning the recording before heading to the field, conducting it with care and clarity in the beneficiary's presence, and uploading it via the system as soon as possible after delivery.

You must never deliver a Bible without recording and submitting the video. The video is not optional — it is the mechanism that closes the accountability loop between the funder, the organization, and the beneficiary. Deliveries without video proof are treated as incomplete operations and will affect your profile's ability to generate new funded requests.

If you cannot record on the delivery day due to exceptional circumstances, document the reason in the operation's Additional Information field and upload the video as soon as possible. Recurring incomplete operations will be reviewed by the organization.

5.3 — Directing the video (the non-negotiable script)

You, the missionary, are the Presenter and Director of this scene. Follow this exact order — do not improvise the sequence.

1

The opening — geographic context

Begin by grounding the donor in the world. They need to know where the Bible arrived.

"Hello! We are here in the city of [City Name], in [Country Name]..."
2

Introducing the beneficiary and the product

Clearly show who is receiving and what they are receiving. Make sure the Bible or Audio Bible is visible on camera, in the person's hands, while you speak.

"...and I am here with [Beneficiary Name], who is receiving their Audio Bible / Printed Bible today."
!

The critical moment — the named acknowledgment

Look directly into the camera and say the exact name of the person who paid. This is the most powerful psychological trigger to retain a donor — hearing their own name called out by you, in the field, with the beneficiary beside you, is what creates the emotional bond that generates future donations. Name everyone — never omit a single name.

"We want to sincerely thank [Donor 1 Name] and [Donor 2 Name] for funding this delivery. Thank you so much for this gift!"
4

The speaking moment — empowering the beneficiary

Turn the phone or microphone toward the beneficiary. This is their moment.

  • If they are shy: a simple "Thank you very much" with a genuine smile is already perfect and sufficient
  • If they want to speak freely: let them share how this will change their life — authenticity is what moves donors the most
  • Never force long speeches, memorized lines, or religious statements if the person is not comfortable
5.4 — Golden tips for audiovisual direction in the field

To ensure the donor can watch the video with good quality, run through this quick checklist before recording:

☀

Light (illumination)

Never record against the sun or a window — the person will be just a dark shadow and the donor won't see the beneficiary's face. The light must be hitting your faces, never coming from behind.

♪

Sound (audio)

Avoid recording near busy highways, loud speakers, or strong wind. The donor needs to hear their own name being called out with total clarity.

▭

Framing

Film from the chest up, close range. The donor wants to see the beneficiary's facial expression and the Bible in their hands. Distant faces create no emotional connection.

5.5 — Complete recording checklist
Identity: I said the full name of the beneficiary who is receiving
The product: I showed and stated what they are receiving — Printed Bible or Audio Bible
Geography: I said the city and country where we are
Named acknowledgment (critical): I thanked all funders directly, calling each one by name Critical
Empowerment: I gave the beneficiary the floor to speak freely
Lighting: the light is hitting our faces, not coming from behind us
Audio: we are away from excessive noise
Framing: we are filming from chest up, at close range
Upload: I confirmed the video was submitted inside the operation in the system
5.6 — The final impact (closing the full cycle)

Action

After recording, click to finalize the video upload inside the operation.

What happens behind the scenes

The system sends a notification directly to the donor's phone and email. They will hear their own name spoken by you and see the beneficiary's face and smile.

  • The donor's trust in you is consolidated
  • The likelihood of that same donor funding your next request has just been multiplied
Executive summary

The digital missionary's creed

To avoid mistakes, commit these two fundamental principles before starting each day:

01

My profile address is locked down and error-free

Because everything will be delivered there. I regularly verify my ZIP code, street, number, and complement. I am the logistics hub — any address error is a lost mission.

02

Bibles reach only those who truly have no other access

Every Bible funded by this platform is intended for someone who has no other way to receive it. If a person can afford or access a Bible on their own but still receives a donated one, it means taking an opportunity away from someone who would have absolutely no access. My commitment is to reach exclusively those who cannot access a Bible by any means — not those who simply didn't have one yet.

Great work in the field! Your organization and digital precision ensure the Word keeps reaching those who need it most.
